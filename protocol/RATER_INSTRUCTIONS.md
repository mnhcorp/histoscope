# Rater instructions

For each panel, identify the main recurring morphological concept or pattern in the displayed patches.

## Morphology

Select one or more terms from the controlled vocabulary that describe the main recurring morphology. Use the free-text field only when the vocabulary does not contain an appropriate term.

## Concept consistency

Rate how consistently the same concept appears across the 12 patches:

| Score | Guidance |
|---:|---|
| 5 | A single, clear concept is present across essentially all patches |
| 4 | The same concept is clear in most patches, with minor variation |
| 3 | A possible shared concept is present, but with substantial variation |
| 2 | Weak or inconsistent evidence of a shared concept |
| 1 | No coherent recurring concept |

## Diagnostic relevance

Rate how useful the identified concept would be in clinical or diagnostic reasoning:

| Score | Guidance |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Highly relevant; a key diagnostic hallmark |
| 4 | Clearly relevant to diagnostic interpretation |
| 3 | Potentially useful contextual morphology |
| 2 | Limited diagnostic value |
| 1 | Not diagnostic; background or artifact |

Raters completed the study independently and were blinded to Histoscope's AUPRC-derived label and score.
