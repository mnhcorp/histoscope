# Feature sampling

The expert study evaluated 100 features from the 49,152-feature SAE dictionary. Sampling was fixed before expert ratings were collected:

- 50 highest-ranked features from the combined monosemantic and nearly-monosemantic groups defined by the prespecified AUPRC rule;
- 50 lowest-ranked features from the polysemantic comparison group.

This confidence-stratified design tested whether the automatic rule was precise at its positive extreme and whether its negative extreme contained coherent morphology. It was not designed to estimate the dictionary-wide prevalence of monosemantic features.

The sampling procedure and example panels are released here. The original 100-feature expert-label dataset and raw rater responses are reserved for a later extended study and are not included.
